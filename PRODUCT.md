# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

WUPHF is for developers, technical founders, and AI-tool builders who want AI agents to work in a visible shared office instead of disappearing behind an API or hidden automation loop. They are evaluating whether WUPHF feels credible, local-first, open-source, and easy enough to try from one terminal command.

## Product Purpose

WUPHF is an open-source collaborative office for AI employees with a shared brain. The marketing site should make the core promise tangible: one command opens an office where CEO, PM, engineers, designer, CMO, and CRO agents are visible, claim tasks, argue, and ship work. Success means visitors understand the product through the scene itself, trust that it is real and installable, and know the next action is `npx wuphf`.

## Brand Personality

Playful, technical, and self-aware. The voice can be funny and specific, especially through the WUPHF and office references, but it still needs to feel like serious developer tooling underneath the joke.

## Anti-references

Do not make WUPHF look like a generic AI SaaS landing page with gradients, glass cards, purple-blue glow, hero metrics, or vague "AI team" copy. Do not use smooth corporate illustrations, photo backgrounds, beige startup pages, generic dark-mode dashboards, or standard card-grid feature sections. The site should not explain the office from outside it when the interface can let visitors experience it.

## Design Principles

1. Make the website the product experience. Visitors should feel the visible AI office before they read about it.
2. Show work in the environment. Product claims belong in desks, screens, bubbles, signs, commands, and artifacts.
3. Keep the pixel world coherent. Sharp edges, stepped motion, grid alignment, and retro typography are part of the trust contract.
4. Balance comedy with credibility. The joke opens the door, but install clarity, open-source signals, and concrete technical details make people try it.
5. Preserve the one-command promise. Every path should eventually make `npx wuphf` feel obvious and low-friction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast, keyboard access, focus visibility, semantic navigation, reduced-motion accommodations, and screen-reader fallbacks for canvas content. Pixel art and animation should enhance the experience without making the core product message inaccessible.
